About Us

Who are we?

Our Vision

Our collective vision is to create a cohesive, caring, sharing and productive alumni family, where people interact regularly and collaborate for the greater good of CETAAS and its members in Singapore

Our Mission

- Provide mutual support for the community well-being and in times of need

- Create a social networking forum where the alumni can play, enjoy, develop and work on various common interests

- Develop a sense of tradition and heritage and support CET’s initiates towards academic excellence

- Assist tertiary institutions in Singapore by way of skills, networking and resources

Our History

CEETAS was formally registered with Registry of Societies in November 2017, establishing a constitution to work towards its stated mission and vision. The first AGM was conducted in December 2017, appointing an Executive Committee authorised to initiate the formation of the Alumni Chapter in Singapore.
CETAAS logo was created and approved by ROS in March 2018. Since its inception, the CEETAS chapter has been making steady progress to bring together our alumni members, estimated to be around 200 people in Singapore. Onam gathering of the Alumni is our yearly highlight event, drawing close to hundred people for traditional sadya and cultural events. Smaller events such as Nature Walks, Cycling Tours and Pot Lucks brings the alumni family together on a more frequent schedule.
CEETAS has been in the forefront of contributing to various initiatives to support CET. In 2020, Singapore Chapter contributed to provide xx laptops to support needy students with infrastructure for e-learning. CEETAS also contributed to the fund raising by the general community for Kerala Flood Relief and Kerala Covid Relief initiatives.
CEETAS considers supporting the alumni and their family members at times of challenges as a the core its mission in Singapore. We encourage any members needing support to reach out to us and we assure you of our best efforts.

History of CET

The College of Engineering, Trivandrum was established in 1939 as the first Engineering College in the then Travancore State. The first classes were started on 3rd July 1939 during the reign of the Travancore King, Sri Chithira Thirunal Balarama Varma and as the head of the then Travancore state he deserves his share of credit in the establishment of the college. Initially the College was housed in the former office and bungalow of the Chief Engineer (present PMG Office).
